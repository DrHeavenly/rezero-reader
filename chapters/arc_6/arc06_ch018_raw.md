It had been a long time since Natsuki Subaru visited this place.

Much like the several previous times, it was a dark world where there was no end to sky and earth, where only darkness resided. It felt like he had been forcibly summoned to this place just like what happened every time he Returned by Death.

If he were to think about it, this too, was a truly mysterious place.

At any rate, it was not like Subaru could pull out his memories of this place, and bring them to the outside. Despite it not being his first time visiting this place, he would forget entering it after leaving.

A transient feeling, just like as if he were a mere bubble floating by in a dream.

Within this pale darkness, in the narrow gaps of his faint recollections, the consciousness known as Natsuki Subaru on the outside floundered through the air, and slowly swam amidst the jet-black darkness.

With only his consciousness, he rose to the surface; of course, regarding his body, he had gotten used to the feeling of not having his limbs, his eyes, his ears, his mouth.

As well as that, there was the fact that he did not know what was top or bottom, left or right, and the anxiety towards not being able to see anything, including the depths below. All of that did not matter to his consciousness, which was dominated by just one thing.

There was an unbearable, unending passion at the core of his consciousness, or perhaps within the formation of his consciousness itself―― no, more so, it was dear love, adoration, deep unconditional love.

Whenever he came here, he could be with the source of this love.

His consciousness had learnt just this thing, and he was delighted that he could visit this place once again. Before he began to even relish that long-awaited encounter, the feeling that pounded in his rightfully nonexistent chest was one of such joy that he wished to open his rightfully nonexistent lips to sing.

However, the passion of his consciousness was――

If neither face nor body existed in this directionless world, then what was the significance in the act of looking behind, was there any? It was not to find a meaning in this, rather it was an act for the sake of giving meaning to it.

To look behind oneself, a body was required. If he consciously thought so, and acted, then the jet-black darkness would have no choice but to give that to the nothingness that was Natsuki Subaru.

Thus, the consciousness which looked back became Natsuki Subaru, now bestowed with vision so he could look around his surroundings, and a body which he could turn back around with.

Yet, that alone was still insufficient, and his body was far off from being complete. This Subaru was an imperfect recreation, the real Natsuki Subaru could not be reproduced at all.

Would it be better if he had hands, or legs, or perhaps a nose and mouth?

――No, making and manifesting a human being out of nothing was not that easy.

Natsuki Subaru did not know nearly enough about Natsuki Subaru to recreate himself.

It was not easy for Natsuki Subaru to complete himself using only his own memories and knowledge.

Therefore, it was impossible currently to make Natsuki Subaru more than this.

There was something other than the Consciousness of Natsuki Subaru coming to the fore within the depths of the darkness.

The heart, the brain of this Natsuki Subaru were not prepared at all to comprehend it. If he were to try and gain an understanding of those words which the Consciousnesses uttered, then just like he materialized some flesh, and just like he had created a sense of sight, he had to bestow upon himself organs to allow him to understand them.

Subaru: “――――”

It was not easy to drag out the material from the immaterial. At the very least, if Natsuki Subaru’s consciousness did not truly wish for it, then he could neither create nor acquire it.

Though Subaru knew he was good at getting up from sleep, on the flipside, he also knew that he found it hard to fall asleep.

For Subaru, waking up from sleep felt as if he were raising his face above the water’s surface. No matter who may be submerged underwater, if their face rose to the water’s surface, they would not forget how to breathe.

For Subaru, that breathing signified the rousing of his consciousness, it was fairly natural that he was able to breathe upon waking up.

This had been Emilia’s comment from when they had this conversation before.

In any case, Subaru was honestly quite envious of how she fell asleep.

Upon lying in bed and closing his eyes in the middle of the night, Subaru wouldn’t stop thinking about all kinds of stuff no matters what he did. In particular, what he would think about included all the things he could have done differently, and all the regrets contained in him.

Whether it was about what happened on that one day, or, sometimes, what happened on days long before, regret would torment Subaru without any heed for either time nor means.

When grappling with stuff like that, he was unable to fall asleep at all. That was the source of his poor quality of sleep.

In addition to that, the chilliness had also faded, and his body was embraced by a feeling quite different to that of lying on top of the sand. A faint look of surprise flitted across Beatrice’s face as she witnessed the strength of Subaru’s reaction, but she kept him at bay by pinching his nose as he drew near to her.

In order to cheer up the downcast Subaru, Beatrice clumsily attempted to urge him into rousing his spirit. Seeing her attempt, Subaru was taken aback, and he jerked his chin up with a “That’s right”,

Subaru ruffled Beatrice’s hair and expressed his gratitude at hearing her sympathetic words.

Beatrice’s hairdo was completely unyielding, and even his attempts at ruffling and disturbing it in this manner were in vain, such was its strength. In any case, under the encouragement of Beatrice, Subaru turned his eyes towards the top of the tower.

It had already been more than a year ago, but he remembered the conversation from back then vividly. He remembered that she had explained it like this, “I think meeting and talking to him will be easier to understand him than talking to each other about him”, in relation to Roswaal.

In reality, she thought that he would not have received the impact unless he met with him in person, and that he would not be on the same wavelength in conversation, but, well, now it was far too late for what happened back then.

Beatrice: [If you meet them, you’ll understand, in fact.]

If he squinted his eyes to where Beatrice’s words had pointed him to, he can just about make out that there’s something like steps―― a staircase, on the outer edge of the circular room indeed. However, a huge number of steps formed the spiral staircase that wound its way around the big, circular tower.

Subaru: [With you telling me to do my best… How many steps are there until the top!?]

Subaru shrieked this out, as it looked likely that he would be made to get some heavy exercise, being made to go up and down all over again. His voice echoed around the tower in vain, most likely it echoed all the way up until to the top before sorrowfully fading away.

Beatrice shrugged her shoulders uttering “Good grief” at Subaru’s lament.

Beatrice: [It can’t be helped, I suppose. If we could use something like a bit of magic for this, it may be possible to fly upstairs in some shape or form, but… Well, Subaru, you have no other choice, in fact.]

Even though Subaru’s physical strength compared to back when he had been a hikikomori[4] was stronger, he was still a feeble boy with quite the ways to go before reaching the standards of strength of this other world, and thus, he could not avoid it.

He didn’t know how high above the Fifth Floor was from the lowermost floor, or rather, the Sixth Floor, but at the very least, he couldn’t even gaze at the ceiling when looking upwards. With how high he thought it was due to that, climbing up would be a deed on the level of mountain climbing.

Subaru: [Damnit! Even so, I have to go up and make sure that the likes of Rem and Patrasche are safe. I have to. Ah, goddamnit.]

Subaru: [Beako?]

Beatrice: [This is bad, I suppose. Subaru, get down, in fact――!]

Beatrice: [Idiot! That doesn’t mean you can pick me up like a baby, I suppose! Get down, in fact!]

Subaru: [It was just on the spur of the moment…]

As he held Beatrice within his arms, Subaru gently leaned his body back. He doesn’t know what’s happening, Beatrice’s alertness was the only thing he had to work from. In preparation for that, he tried to think, but it was too late.

The source of that pressure came ever closer with ferocity, and in the next moment something blasted towards Subaru, right in front of his eyes―― a violent quake shook the enormous tower, Subaru was nearly blown away by the shockwave which raised sand and dust up.

He would have probably been thrown about over and over if not for Gian’s enormous frame just behind them. Protected by the weight of the ground dragon and the dragon carriage, Subaru desperately pushed his eyes open in the midst of the sandstorm that was blowing against him.

And then, on the other side of the smoke plume, from the epicenter of the impact the cloud of sand and dust that had risen up abruptly cleared.

Something like a gust of wind tore through the smoke, and soon after, from the other side, the majestic figure of a person could be seen. Tearing through the dense sandy haze which had enveloped them, the person’s figure was turning visible. Subaru’s cheeks stiffened, he was frozen in place.

That figure, their appearance, he recognized it.

Subaru: [You are…?]

A tall woman was walking briskly across the hard floor, stepping ever closer to him.

Her brown, closer to black, hair was tied up into a ponytail; she was marching forward boldly, her limbs, belly and back were exposed by her clothing; it was safe to say that she was half-naked. Her chest and crotch area were just barely covered up, and she donned a black mantle over her outfit. She was a person who wore rather odd clothing.

In Subaru-speak, she looked like a nympho who was donning her mantle over a black bikini top and hot pants.

Long, slender, white arms and legs, along with a bountiful chest that swung about liberally. She was around the same height as Subaru, maybe a little taller. The size of her hips however, could not be compared.

With a handsome face sat on top of her white shoulders, she was quite the looker, with eyes that albeit languid, were rather striking.
