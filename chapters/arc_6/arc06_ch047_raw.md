You can only be yourself. Others can only be themselves.

There was no room for compromise, no compromise out of pity, no chance to engender a compromise, no being considerate to each other, there was nothing, nothing at all in the end.

Emilia: [Subaru…]

Subaru: [――――]

Shaking his head back and forth, he exerted himself in trying to extract the thorn of the so-called “other person” from his own inside with utmost effort.

Whilst feeling traces of the two’s worries on their cheeks, the boy―― the individual who should be called Natsuki Subaru was attempting to determine the boundary between his own self and others. A swirl of thoughts clouded him, the correct answer disappeared due to the whirl of emotions melting together inside him.

His lungs were convulsing; if he were to feel his throat now, he would realize that his breathing had become painful. From within his neck, he was experiencing a severe sense of pressure; a fear that he would suffocate was slowly assaulting him.

The sensation of impending Death once again revived within him, the feeling of wanting to vomit surfaced inside him and caused warm tears to stream out of his eyes.

Subaru: [――――]

While confronted by this horrible feeling, Subaru slowly and carefully tried to exclude that which would not define him from within him, struggling to establish his own personal identity.

First person pronouns, second person pronouns, memory, recollections, impressions, feelings, identifying that which belonged to the other person, and distinguishing between them.

Just like that, if he sifted through what was not his and removed the stuff that had gotten blended together, he would go back to as he was.

Otherwise, this state, in which they were fused and blended together, would never peel off.

It was himself, and the little girl who had been killed by his own “hands” that were mixed together into a hodgepodge of――

“Anastasia”: [The little girl who accompanied us is missing. Furthermore, a Book which seems to match with the little girl’s name was found in this Library which records the memories of the dead… This situation is serious. We cannot afford to be treading water here.]

Emilia: [That’s… Yeah, but…]

Emilia was incapable of finding a rebuttal against Echidna’s low, insisting tone of voice.

In this messed-up situation, the only one to possess vital information was Subaru. Taking everyone’s feelings into consideration, it could be said that everyone here had no time to spare to calm down.

As she knew that, Echidna was prepared to be blamed, so with a steeled heart, she raised her questions at Subaru.

At hearing her resolve, Subaru grit his teeth again and placed his hand against his throat.

With all his strength, fighting against the feeling that he could not breathe, he shut his eyes. And――

“Anastasia”: [Let me try asking again, Natsuki-kun. What did you see inside the bo――]

Subaru: [――I saw, Meili’s, memories…]

“Anastasia”: [――――]

Subaru responded with perfect honesty to Echidna’s questions without attempting to deceive her.

Just as he said that, to her side, Emilia held her breath, and in front of him, Echidna closed her eyes. Next to them, Beatrice, Julius and Ram each respectively had expressions of worry and grief floating across their faces.

“The Book of the Dead had shown Subaru Meili’s memories.

Could there have been a mistake, and the memories in Meili’s Book had been laid out there by some error? How easy would it have been to comfort oneself by clinging onto such futile hope.

However, in this place, he was not alone in not wanting to surrender himself to this deception and escaping towards the more comfortable option.

Especially for the girls right next to Subaru whose cheeks had stiffened in distress trying to bear this.

Beatrice had so benevolently said that it had not been Subaru’s fault.

Julius: […It is bitter, but, I have no basis to reject the words of Miss Ram. Yesterday, when I acted in this predicament so selfishly and arbitrarily without consulting anyone… I am definitely not qualified to do this at all.]

Echidna raised her hand and barged her way into the conversation after hearing Julius’s self-deprecating words.

And, whilst fiddling with the white scarf wrapped around the base of her neck,

“Anastasia”: [However, the basis of my argument is not the state of mind Julius is in. Purely, I am only stating my opposition to this because, from looking at the state Natsuki-kun is in, it is too dangerous to try this.]

As Ram and Julius wrack their brains, Echidna nods at the two with an “Ahh”,

“Anastasia”: [Simply put, could there be a mechanism where the burden increases the more Books you read…? Or, perhaps, could it be that it warps your mind due to seeing the memories of a person that was close to you? I think the possibilities are divided into two.]

Most likely, Echidna’s guesses were correct.

People involved in closer relationships―― those fresh records of Life would bring a greater impact.

It was natural that people would be more interested in close companions than in people they had more distant relationships with.

Subaru also wished this as a fact. As a matter of fact, Subaru, and Meili’s minds had almost merged together, and he was coming to grips with that he wanted to know her.

As a result, Subaru had come to face the unfathomable emptiness in the depth of “Oneself”.

That young girl had been carrying this feeling of emptiness for all of her life――

Subaru: [Search…?]

Emilia’s words were not solid, they were far off from being reliable and wise.

Her neck had been strangled, her consciousness had slipped away as she was being choked, her thoughts had been filled with hatred and despair, and then it had stopped abruptly―― it must have been in that very moment that her life had ceased.

It was Natsuki Subaru who had experienced this and had been complicit in hiding the girl’s corpse.

Natsuki Subaru was helping the man who killed “me”. The Natsuki Subaru who knew “me”, was helping none other than “Natsuki Subaru”, the most detestable in this world.

Emilia, Beatrice, Echidna and Julius’s eyes had turned towards Subaru.

His thoughts scuzzy, Subaru was only struggling to frantically protect himself and his hands stained with blood. He possessed such an ugly, rotten heart; that was what it seemed like.

Then, better yet, listen to “my” desires――

Subaru: [――I didn’t see up until the point where she died. There’s no doubt though that she’s inside the Tower though.]

Dangerous feelings were sprouting up deep down inside of him―― she was trying to convince Subaru to confess to his crime. That was the sweet curse left behind by Meili Portroute.

These thoughts were certainly newly born within Subaru.

They wanted Emilia and the others to find Meili’s dead body. Find her, mourn her, repent for what had happened, make him feel sorrow; the thoughts inside of him wanted to relieve the emotions coiled within his heart.

He was getting to the point where he too no longer knew who out of Natsuki Subaru, “myself”, and “Natsuki Subaru” desired what.

And amidst Subaru’s self-condemnation and the awkward atmosphere left behind by Shaula.

A gemstone that only Natsuki Subaru could have, that only he could gaze upon.

Translation notes:
[1] Not to detract from the reading experience, but there are some important nuances in this chapter you have to be aware of which are hard to do in English. Putting a preamble makes me avoid putting in tons of footnotes. As you have seen last Chapter, reading Meili’s book caused Subaru to sort of take in her personality. She has a distinct style of speaking, using this form to refer to herself: わたし (Watashi), whereas Subaru tends to use 俺 (ore). The way I have chosen to represent this is by using italics to represent when Meili interjects in his thoughts. So any passages in italics should correspond to Meili’s voice inside Subaru’s head. In addition, I preserve the self-referencing word (I/myself/my/etc.) in the Japanese quotation marks. Also, WordPress allow it, but the title of the Chapter is said using Meili’s pronoun, so it should be in italics.
[2] This whole bit of narration in italics is Subaru’s internal thoughts, which are usually represented in italics, and somewhat conflicts with the representation of Meili’s pronoun. The only instance in which Meili interjects is this last sentence.
