Because leaving Julius behind, here, by himself, was not an option.

Subaru: [Vaguely speaking, we’re halfway down some stairs, a bit more precisely speaking, we’re halfway down a long staircase, and even more precisely, we’re halfway fleeing down the staircase between the Second and Fourth Floors.]

Subaru: [Yes, I am. And let me just say that this is the second time this has happened in this short period of time. I’m having to do this again thirty minutes after I’d thought I’d never like to do this again; do you get how I am feeling about this?]

Subaru: [Do you want me to throw you off?]

Subaru could sense Julius’s wheezed laughter coming from his back. He ignored the sarcasm in his words and untensed himself a little.

In all honesty, Subaru had not gandered any predictions at what Julius’s first words after waking up. He would not be exaggerating if he said that he had even been fearing what he would say, truthfully speaking.

Subaru: […I’m not some kind of demon who’d blame you for losing against an opponent like that.]

Julius sounded awfully like himself, and for that Subaru let out a sigh and started going down the staircase once more.

Having regained his consciousness, Julius was much easier to carry than before. He had also cleared up some of his anxieties which had been ladening his step, so thanks to that he could tackle the remaining steps with renewed vigor.

Julius: [――Reid Astrea.]

Subaru: [――――]

His voice was brimming with confidence in his response. Subaru was so surprised for a second that both his feet and breath caught in their tracks. However, he quickly brought both his feet and breath back into motion so to not let him onto his surprise.

Subaru carefully chose his words so that Julius would not sense his flustered state.

Subaru: [So, so you knew all along? That thing. Shaula said she knows him. Makes sense that she does because she seems acquainted with him from four-hundred years ago… But, it looks like she was telling the truth when she alluded that he’s someone that’s hard to deal with. I say that since she fainted right after she saw his face.]

He did not even let himself swing his sword against me. I should just simply say that he is a powerful person. Even the term “Stick Swinger” is one that denotes him, from what I have seen in literature.]

Julius: [Actually, the nuance of it is that he did not choose a weapon he specialized with. I had my thoughts about him when he introduced himself as a Stick Swinger… But, I was unsure. I apologize for not mentioning them to you.]

Subaru had no reply for Julius’s apologetic words. Though he had stated that there had been tons of things which had tipped him off, Subaru thought it too much of a stretch for him.

Reid Astrea, the man who was known as the First Sword Saint, should have died four-hundred years ago.

Just because some of his features matched did not mean that one could easily come to the conclusion of having encountered that hero of yore whose name had gone down in fairytale and legend.

If there had been a chance that he could have noticed that, then he, Subaru, should have been able to come to that conclusion.

After all, he and Emilia had both agreed that the Trials which had been put forward by this Watchtower were similar to the Trials they had taken at Echidna’s Tomb.

With that said, Subaru should have given more thought about the contents of the Trials set out by this Tower and raised as many possibilities as he could.

Neglecting that had led to their routing on the Second Floor Electra.

Julius: [Hmm. How in the world did we end up coming across this swordsman of legend brought back from the past…? Originally, I would have been delighted in having been granted this boon, but all things considered…]

Subaru: [I get how you feel. These heroes of legend are all good at being disappointing in reality. This Watchtower is full of let downs, like with Shaula who’d been judged as a Sage.]

Julius: […You get, how I feel…?]

His voice was cracked and it felt full of self-derision.

Subaru inconsiderately gritted his teeth upon hearing what Julius had moaned out from up-close. But he chose not to touch on that, opting instead to say, “Even so”.

Subaru: [If I’m recalling correctly, the First Sword Saint is engraved on gold coins, but there’s a pretty big difference between that and the actual guy. Shaula also differed in her gender, so even though we can’t do much about it, there’s a considerable gap between them two too. The guy on the gold coin looked more like a middle-aged dude…]

Julius: [Just a quick historical tidbit, it was when he was older that Reid accomplished his deeds that led him to be counted as one of the Three Great Heroes. The figure depicted on the gold coins is probably correct. The him up above is younger than the one from recorded history.]

Subaru: [Come to mention it, it didn’t seem that Reid recognized Shaula…]

He had planned to change the subject, but per chance they ended up touching on another issue.

Compared to what Julius had said, there had definitely been too much of a difference of hot and cold between the two of Reid and Shaula for there to have been some sort of friendship between them. If he assumed that “This guy was the Reid before he’d met Shaula”, then there would be some parts he could agree with regarding the two’s differences in recognizing each other.

But, when he took into consideration Shaula’s overreaction and the high likelihood that Reid had not remembered her name or face due to how he was, the basis of his hypothesis weakened.

Subaru: [If we take that as correct, doesn’t that mean that they would have fought against the Witch after their prime days? Meaning we’d have to beat Reid in his prime.]

Julius: [Feels to me like that is going to be either really tough, or nigh-impossible.]

Subaru: [Yeah, for sure it’s gonna be an exhausting mess. But I’m sure that there’ll be some way of getting around this if we come up with a plan. As things stand…]

Right now, at this moment, he should have told Julius what was weighing on his mind, but he had not because his heart had stopped him in his tracks.

Yet he had been a little too slow.

Julius: [Subaru.]

Subaru: [That’s all. The reason why she beat the guy down wasn’t due to her being stronger than him. Let’s just say that lady luck was at her side… Because Emilia is well, unique like that.]

Emilia had just barely had her resolve and ability recognized by their Trial taker, Reid, in the Trial. However, the circumstances that had led to her achieving that were hard to explain to anyone who had not witnessed them.

Because, even if they tried to beat him in the same way, it would not be possible by anyone other than Emilia.

Julius: [Yeah, yeah, everything is fine. I am listening of course… I see, Emilia-sama managed it.]

Julius had unexpectedly accepted the fact that Emilia had passed the Trial rather flexibly. Subaru had nervously explained it to him, but he sensed that Julius was sidestepping his question from his reaction―― No, things were fine.

But it was pointless for Julius to worry himself sick over that. Subaru could say that he was taking a bit too much of a naive look at him. Or by his own measure, he guessed that he should not have leapt to conclusions about the Knight named Julius Juukulius.

Reid’s blows had been the kind that had been devised to tear through Julius’s heart.

As soon as Subaru saw that Julius hesitated in going against him and that, for now, the latter had lost the strength to shake him off as he had been doing, he started to walk again, whilst still lending his shoulder to Julius. Whether he liked it or not.

Julius had no time to go against him, now that he had set the pace like that.

Subaru: [I’m not going to act like I know or understand what’s going through your heart.]

Julius: [――――]

Subaru: [But, right now, you needn’t walk down these stairs, these long-ass stairs alone, all by yourself. I will lend you my shoulder and I won’t think of it as a debt either.]

Talking about lending, borrowing and debts was ridiculous.

If he put it like that, then how much did he owe Julius?

Surely his first debt to him would have started in the Training Ground at the Royal Castle.

――He knew the reason why Julius had challenged Reid, even though he knew victory was not reachable.

He had… he had acted the same as Subaru back then.

Back then, Subaru had challenged Julius even though he was aware a win was unattainable. No matter how many times he had been knocked down, no matter how many times he had been struck, he had picked himself up without learning his lesson and kept challenging him.

Because, back then, he had no other way to hurl out the tempestuous emotions that had built up inside his heart than that.

And, back then, Subaru had ended up “alone”, at a place where everything had ended for him, at a place where he had argued with Emilia and where she had bid him her goodbyes. That had been agonizing. That had made him want to cry.

――So how could he leave Julius all alone on this staircase.

His heart must be set ablaze. Just like him back then.

But unlike him, he still did not know where he could hurl out his tempestuous emotions.

Julius: [――Subaru.]

And like that, the two of them slowly climbed down the stairs, and made it back to the Fourth Floor.
