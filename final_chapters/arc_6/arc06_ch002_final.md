# Chapter 2 — His Way of Providing a Warm Welcome

It was a little late to be noting it, but the Emilia Camp’s base had been moved to the main residence of the Mathers Territory—a different building from the mansion that had burned to the ground.

Even so, the layout hardly differed from the old one. It was arranged in the same familiar way: the main building set in the center, the west and east wings raised to either side, and the whole of it kept to roughly the same scale, so that no one would lose their bearings wandering it. The only trouble was that it was a touch too similar, which made it all too easy to forget that Arlam Village no longer stood nearby. More than once Subaru had sprung out of bed full of energy, meaning to go do his morning calisthenics in the village, only to realize somewhere along that long road that he no longer knew which way to turn.

Beatrice would come chasing after him in a fluster, and she would be very angry.

At any rate, they had reached Roswaal’s mansion, been welcomed by the same old Petra and Ram, and stepped into the parlor where the master of the house awaited them—but—

“Ohhh~, welcome baaack. So glad you all made it home safe and souuund.”

Roswaal sat on the couch clapping his hands, welcoming them home. Subaru and Emilia both flinched, taken aback by the beaming reception. They looked at that excessive, amiable smile, then looked at each other.

“…Emilia-tan, was there anything strange written in that letter?”

“I-I wouldn’t know, all right. And besides—you didn’t go and prepare some strange present, did you? Because, you know, Subaru and Roswaal have their secret talks sometimes, so…”

“N-no way! And even if I were planning to give Ros-chi a present, I’d fold in my usual thanks and consult Emilia-tan, Beatrice, Petra, Frederica, Patrasche, and maybe even Ram about how to cut down the time and money it’d cost.”

“Those were the names of girls, one and all, I suppose.”

“Isn’t it embarrassing to ask guys like Otto and Garfiel for advice on a present from one man to another?!”

Subaru answered the sulky Beatrice, then sent a wry smile back at Emilia’s scornful eyes. Incidentally, he had deliberately left Rem’s name off the list, so as not to breed unease in the others or invite a misunderstanding—though he did feel a pang of guilt over it. In any case, Roswaal, who had watched the whole exchange, gave a shrug.

“Whaaat a surprising reaction. I worry over your safety, so I wonder what’s so strange about being glaaad you’re all home in one piece. It’s only natural, isn’t it?”

“That’s… true, but… Roswaal, you’ve never really shown us this side of yourself before, so…”

“It meaaans my way of thinking has gone through all sorts of changes this past yeeear. Shouldn’t Emilia-sama be a little pleased that I’ve turned cooperative?”

“Hmm. You’re right.”

Emilia answered his brazen turnabout in her usual tolerant way, accepting his change of heart. He nodded at her, and just watching her wave her palms back and forth left Subaru unable to leave the trust at so thin a level—but—

“Even if you tried to make sense of the way he thinks, you couldn’t, because it’s far too twisted, in fact. Subaru… Subaru’s a little like that too, but the like of that is more, I suppose.”

“You’re being way too vague with your words.”

Stroking the head of Beatrice, who seemed to have decided to fold something vague and gnomic into her remark, Subaru stepped on into the parlor after Emilia.

Roswaal in high spirits—they had their doubts, but they were dimly aware of the motive under it all. In fact, he’d all but stated the motive himself a moment ago: Roswaal truly was glad that Subaru and Emilia had come home safe. Now that the Book of Wisdom was no longer his, amiability was the strongest move he had left to serve the purpose he had yet to reveal. His single aim was for Subaru’s actions to break down the obstacles in the way.

“If that’s how you feel, then fine. Though it’d help if you were a little cooperative yourself.”

“You’ll reach your goal with or without my help. Which is why I believe my cooperation is a needless addition to your strength. It really isn’t an eeeven trade between us.”

“It feels like I’m stuck with one of those RPG setups where you’ve got an amazing support character you’re just not allowed to use, it really does.”

It was the kind of arrangement where the absurdly powerful support would do anything except join the fight. Roswaal’s refusal to take the field was much the same—in his case, he’d only grow more and more entertained, sitting back to watch Subaru, a man who believed in his own power to change fate through Return by Death, throw himself into desperate measures and drive himself deeper and deeper into a corner. A cunning fellow, and the sort who made Subaru want to click his tongue.

“—Ain’t it ’bout time ya introduced us too?”

“Sorry to keep you waiting. Roswaal, we’ve got visitors. Let them take a seat.”

“So I hear. Interesting faces, all the same.”

The girl who’d been waiting out in the corridor called in to the pair talking things over at the doorway. Subaru offered a light apology and gestured toward Roswaal on the couch, signaling him to let them in. Roswaal rose, then settled back into the head seat without a sound, and directed the two guests to the visitors’ sofa. Subaru and the rest slowly took their places on the couch facing him.

“Welcome, from all that distance. The last time we spoke face-to-face like this was at the Recognition Ceremony.”

“That’s true. And it’s not as if we’d had a proper conversation even then… so this practically feels like the first real talk between us.”

The Recognition Ceremony that had come up was the formal acknowledgment of the White Whale’s subjugation, an achievement of the combined Emilia, Anastasia, and Crusch Camps. Since the small ceremony had been held at the Royal Castle in the capital, every member free to attend right after the Sanctuary affair was settled had naturally been present, the camps sending their representatives to gather. Subaru had vivid memories of Otto’s tense, pale face, and of the castle shining in Garfiel’s eyes. Roswaal had blithely decided to attend, even after all the trouble he’d put them through. What a man. Well—Subaru had been nervous himself, sure, but he couldn’t say Emilia had been any steadier; she’d fidgeted worse than he had, and no wonder, praised as she was for feats she had no memory of accomplishing.

Anyway, that was a gathering of the past. The exchange of greetings was carried off in short order, and the conversation moved at once to the main matter: a report on the city of Priestella—

“—And just as the letter said, Otto-kun and Garfiel have withdrawn on account of their injuries. I see, undeerstood. Truly promising work, all of it.”

A sigh of admiration slipped out of Roswaal at the end of the report. That made Emilia lift her eyebrows and put in a “Hey,” a hard edge to her voice.

“They were hurt so badly they couldn’t even come home, and you still put it like that. What are you trying to say?”

“Let’s not leap tooo hastily to any conclusion. Yes, it’s heartbreaking that they were wounded so, and their forced, temporary withdrawal is no less heartbreaking than the wounds themselves. But on the other hand—no one in the camp took fatal harm. Set against the other camps, and considering you crossed paths with more than one Sin Archbishop… indeed, this outcome is nothing short of miraculous.”

“—”

“The scars the Sin Archbishops left behind should of course be tended without delay. But Emilia-sama, and everyone who fought to protect that city, did their utmost to keep the damage to the barest minimum. I do think that’s something you ought to recognize in yourseelf.”

“…Oh. Thanks.”

The look on Emilia’s face was that of someone unconvinced, and she gave her thanks all the same. In truth, his words should have carried the ring of sound reasoning. But so much depended on one’s impression of the speaker—and Subaru thought him shady, as, most likely, did all the rest.

“Weell, setting aside what everyone makes of me—shall we move to the next thing? Incidentally, the one who summoned Emilia-sama to Priestella, where all that uproar broke out, was Anastasia-sama, was it not…? What have you to say for it?”

“It’s inexcusable, draggin’ her into that mess. If ya want me to apologize, I’ll do it properly. I’ve prepared myself for it.”

“And if you bow your head so sincerely, this ends without your paying back a single diime… oh, but there’s no need to apologize to me. Emilia-sama has already turned you down, hasn’t she?”

“Because—shouldn’t the blame fall on the Witch Cult, if they’re the ones who caused the trouble? Anastasia shouldn’t have to answer for anything; she only invited us there. And it’s not as though we failed to accomplish what we were summoned to Priestella to do…”

Roswaal slid his gaze sideways, and in answer Emilia touched the pendant at her throat. A brand-new, sizable spellstone was set into it. At this very moment, a Great Spirit slept quietly within, awaiting revival. To obtain that gem of the highest purity, the stone that would become Puck’s Anchor—that had been the camp’s goal, and it had been achieved.

“And besides, isn’t it possible the Witch Cult was driven off simply because we happened to be there? Looked at that way, it should count as Anastasia’s achieveme—”

“To read it like that’d be far too generous to another camp, so I’ll stop ya there.”

For some reason, it was Anastasia being rescued now, by Emilia’s optimism. But Roswaal’s line of questioning was only meant to keep everyone in check. In fact, it was entirely plausible that most of the Witch Cult’s targets had been brought into Priestella by Subaru and the others. Point that out, and the mood would surely turn heavy with the Cult’s evil. So it was in everyone’s interest to lock in a single stance: Let’s work together. The Witch Cult is the worst.

“The other three camps have already agreed on that. One Sin Archbishop killed, one captured… man, it was one thing after another with the Witch Cult.”

“Their organizational skills are a touch dubious, though. In their case, they’re a pack of outlaws with no acquaintance with words like ‘cooperation’ or ‘unity.’ Even if one of them were the last left standing, every ally lost, that lone lunatic’s morale wouldn’t dip in the sliightest.”

“…I can’t argue with that.”

Roswaal’s read matched Subaru’s own, which probably meant it was the right one. The so-called “organization” of the Witch Cult was less an organization than a ragtag gang of madmen. And there was no chance they’d sit quiet and obedient from here on. The brutality of the remaining Gluttony and Lust would never be stopped.

“That’s why we need a way to fight back. The next thing I want to talk about ties into that.”

“…Making contact with the Sage Shaula, hm.”

“I know it won’t be easy. But there’s no choice except to try, as long as we can’t come up with anything else. An all-powerful, all-knowing—or well, let’s just say all-knowing. If the Sage really is like that, there’s no harm in going to talk to her, right?”

“Aaalthough, everyone in the past few hundred years who’s tried that came away paying a heavy price—with their liives.”

He set out the common knowledge, and a disturbing view of the matter it was. But it was for precisely that reason they had a card up their sleeve. When Subaru traded a look with her, Anastasia gave a slight nod of the chin in reply, and then,

“That’s where I come in. Lucky for us, I’ve got a way to reach the Sage’s Watchtower safe and sound. So there’s no need to fret about losin’ the road. Does that ease yer worries any?”

“Well now, should I truly take your word for it just becaause…? To begin with, if you really held such a method, there’d be no shortage of people willing to pay a fortune to learn it. So why are you the only one who knows?”

“Money matters plenty to me, as a merchant—but there are things in this world money can’t stand in for. This is one of ’em.”

Hand pressed to her slender chest, Anastasia—no, Eridna—declared it with a flourish. For all that she was only playing at being Anastasia, her words carried a strange power, a power that astonished even Subaru, who knew this was not her true self. Faced with that Anastasia, Roswaal answered by closing one eye. Fixing her for a moment with a single yellow eye, he said,

“Ohhh myyy. This is just why those who understand both the fight and the gamble are so hard to handle. And I’m sure Emilia-sama has already agreed to all of this, anywaay.”

“I’m sorry for making these decisions all on my own, okay?”

“But you’ll never stop making them of your own accord. Which is fine. You’ll dare to walk this thorny road—and that’s where he comes into the picture, too.”

Roswaal fired the retort back half in jest, though he was plainly curious how they meant to get there. The higher the hurdle Emilia chose, the higher the one Subaru would have to clear in turn. For to Roswaal, Subaru was now the hope that had replaced the Book of Wisdom.

“Anyway, going by Anastasia’s information, we’ll hit some kind of desert. Though it’ll take a few days.”

“It is not ‘some kind of desert,’ it is the Augria Sand Dunes. You’ve had that corrected any number of times already, so how about you finally commit it to memory correctly?”

Julius put in his own two words, letting out a sigh at Subaru’s hazy recollection. The man who had sat in silence beside Anastasia this whole time turned his keen gaze on Roswaal and went on,

“I do believe this may be a most difficult proposal for you to accept, Roswaal-sama. But in the city of Priestella there remain a great many who were laid bare to the Witch Cult’s savagery, and the number still wounded in body and mind alike is vast. If there is any action we might take that could serve as their salvation, then I would beg leave to take it.”

“You have quiite the graceful way with words. It seems you’re the owner of some extraordinary gifts… and yet it makes no sense that I have no recollection of them. Which means—that. Riight?”

“—”

Roswaal grasped the situation from his own nonexistent memories of the man. Julius’s gaze fell, and Roswaal propped his chin on the hand of the arm resting on the armrest. Then, loosening his lips into something cheerful, he said,

“Everyone’s forgotten you, on account of the Authority of Gluttony. As though the world itself has left you behind. And to break out of the bind you’re in, you mean to chase the thin thread of hope that is the Sage Shaula. There’s no need to dress it up as being for everyone else’s sake, you knoow.”

“—Kh. I am by no means doing this for selfish reasons!”

“I’m not blaming yoou. It’s a perfectly natural thing—one I find rather preferable, in fact. To do anything at all for your own sake, over doing next to nothing for another’s, is a desperate act. And if others happen to be saved by it, while you take your own satisfaction and sense of accomplishment from the deed, there’s no need to deny that condescending heart of yours.”

Julius’s face went rigid as Roswaal battered him with word after word. Beneath the clown’s paint, his smile only deepened.

“And more than that—if you save yourself, there’s every chance others will be saved along with you. You even carry a just cause to act on. So there’s no need to shoulder the least little blame, is there?”

“I—”

“—Margrave Mathers, might I ask you to stop there?”

Anastasia checked Julius with her voice, stepping in to face Roswaal in his place. She let a graceful smile rise and tilted her lovely head. And then,

“I’ve got no memories of him either, but… even so, this boy seems to be my knight. Which is why it doesn’t sit right, watchin’ him bullied and doin’ nothin’ ’bout it.”

“You’re saying the bond of master and servant still holds between the two of you, even with him gone from your memory…?”

“I wonder. I’m not too sure of that myself. Though it did feel natural, and pleasant, that Julius looked after me on the road… and besides—”

Anastasia gently raised a finger and pointed toward the sofa across from her.

“I’d rather not see ya tear yer own camp apart, if ya keep that up. Ya follow?”

“—My, oh my.”

At Anastasia’s point—he glanced at Subaru, on the verge of eruption, and promptly shrugged. Subaru was a breath from shouting. And naturally, Emilia and Beatrice were on the same cusp. Raising both hands in surrender, Roswaal made his statement.

“All right, all right. My apologies. Thooough I do think I was only pointing out that a certain possibility exists.”

“That was uncalled for. You really need to quit screwing around.”

“Was it, though? You’ll most likely run into people who don’t care in the least how many benefit from the outcome, and into distrustful comrades who drag you down with venomous complaints. You didn’t seem to have that possibility in mind, so I merely thought to advise you to anticipaate it.”

Not doing it for another’s sake, but for one’s own. As it happened, Subaru had a history of being lashed for exactly that—one that remained in him as a raw wound. It was what he had told Emilia at the Royal Castle: that he was acting for her sake, and her answer had been that he was acting for his own. He would never forget the ache that followed. That much was certain. But whether it had been true or not, there were plenty who’d raise it only to wound him. And that didn’t mean that—

“It doesn’t make your achievements worth any less, how many people approve of what you do. Like this guy’s one to talk. Cut it out.”

“It can’t be helped, riight? Because unlike you, I’m not acquainted with his character… though your relationship with him doesn’t look like the sort you’d build over a few days on the road.”

Roswaal turned to Subaru, who was on the edge of losing his temper. In that one moment, his eyes, framed by their long lashes, clouded over with a full-blown sorrow. Then,

“Which means you’re the only one who remembers, once again. Just like with Rem.”

“Though I do wonder what kind of fate this is.”

“It’s proof that your existence is a special one. You should cherish it. Treasure iit—because there is much in this world that can’t be had, however hard one wishes for it.”

No one but Roswaal himself followed the latter half of that feeling-laden line. But even if Subaru pressed him with question after question until he shed light on it, Roswaal would never let the truth out; so Subaru quickly gave the matter up. In any case, the things that needed saying should have been said by now. Next would be—

“Just like the letter said—the Confinement Room, and…”

“And the Sleeping Beauty will be brought out, yes. What a drastic step. I’d have thought you truly hated the idea of anyone laying a hand on her.”

“There might be a way to wake her, so I’ll try it. Isn’t that obvious?”

“I was surprised you’d try this measure with that girl first, of all people. You give the impression of a selfish man, but the truth is you punish yourself. Somewhere in your heart, in some corner of your mind, you’ve believed from the very start that she should be the first to be saved—haven’t you?”

“—”

He’d struck the bullseye, and Subaru sank into silence in spite of himself. From the time they’d set out toward the Pleiades Watchtower to the moment they reached the mansion, Subaru had been asking himself, over and over, whether bringing Rem along as one of Gluttony’s victims was truly the right course. It wasn’t that he had any reluctance to wake her—not in the least. If there was any chance she might wake, he wanted her to wake as soon as it could happen, even for a single day, a single second. But whether it should be Natsuki Subaru, of all people, to do it—that was another matter.

All those people in Priestella bore the same kind of wound as Rem. Theirs was a state in which the sleepers weren’t so much as counted by their own families, who had no idea their loved ones’ very existence had been stolen. The more Subaru turned it over, the more he felt a strange wedge driven into his heart. It was the guilt of saving her ahead of a multitude of others. That was why he’d hesitated this time over bringing Rem out—but—

“I’ve already talked Subaru around, so there’s no problem.”

“…My my, this is quite the surprise.”

In place of the silence-struck Subaru, the one answering Roswaal was Emilia. Turning his gaze to her, to the chest she’d puffed out with pride, Roswaal quizzically shut one eye.

“You say that, but wouldn’t her waking be a trouble for you, Emilia-sama? By any measure, Subaru-kun holds strong feelings for that girl. As strong, in fact, as the feelings he holds for you…”

“Yes, I think that’s right. Subaru will pour himself into that girl for a while, and he might stop paying attention to me, too.”

“No, there is no way in any circumstance that would—”

Happen. It must not happen. His feelings for Emilia had never once wavered, and he swore they never would. And yet another part of him held fierce feelings for Rem. That was a fact—and just as Emilia said, if she woke, there was no doubt he would give himself over to her.

But Emilia turned to Subaru with an “It’s fine,” and went on,

“If it turns Subaru away from me, then it just means I’ll be the one to try my hardest to turn him back my way. I’d be in real trouble if Subaru weren’t here, after all that. So I’ll have you come to me too, no matter how madly in love you are with Rem and how cute she is.”

“Eh? Emilia-tan?!”

“That’s my resolve, and that’s what I’ve decided. And no one will fault you for saving her—so it’s fine. Let’s wake Rem up.”

Emilia braced Subaru’s decision with strong words. Somehow they sounded almost like a confession, and the surprise of it set his knees trembling. Emilia had told him things that sounded like affection on many an occasion—that much was true—but never once had she gone past the line of deep affection—

“Me, Rem, Beatrice, Petra, Patrasche, Otto-kun, Garfiel—we all want you to be reaaally, reaaally happy!”

“You kind of mixed a ground dragon and some grown men into the back half of that, though.”

She’d slipped a loli into the middle, too. In any case, he took it in—and then Beatrice, seated beside him, poked his shoulder. An “Oh, uh” escaped him as he looked to his side, where Beatrice wore her usual disgruntled face.

“It’s not as though I mean to complain about Subaru’s habit of fooling around with the girls… but one of his hands ought to be free at all times, I suppose. This is Betty’s exclusive special privilege, in fact.”

“…You’re ridiculously cute.”

“That’s a given, I suppose. Betty’s adorableness resounds to the gods of heaven and earth, in fact.”

He didn’t much care what the gods of heaven and earth made of the matter, but it did resound in Subaru’s heart. Backed by both Emilia and Beatrice, Subaru would see Rem woken. He no longer wavered over doing it.

“So that’s how it is, Roswaal. Sorry for being loved so much. Rem’s getting brought out.”

“You’ll only startle Emilia-sama and Beatrice-sama, making them sound like some pair of runners-up like that. It’s fine, do as you please. I never meant to stop you anywaay.”

“Right, then—back to that talk from before.”

“I was only feeling some concern, and wanted to make sure you understood the meaning of what you’re doing. And I did wound your camp’s remarkable, nameless knight—my apologies for that.”

“It’s fine.”

Roswaal spoke as though it were all banter to the very end. Julius, for his part, drew a small breath and looked from Anastasia to Subaru and back.

“I am Julius Juukulius. For now this remains only in his memory, but I am a Royal Guard of the Kingdom of Lugunica. I am not so poor a man that my heart would be shaken by something of this order.”

He made the declaration in fine style, brushing aside the badgering of the wickedly evil mage with a splendid stroke.

“…Not very convincing.”

“Whose side are you on, exactly? I feel as though I’ve just been stabbed in the back.”

He slipped that last line in the moment the conversation had been brought to its close.

The talk had ended, and Subaru and the others had left the parlor.

They were bound for the Confinement Room in the mansion’s east wing. There they would come face-to-face with someone who might well be one of the keys to reaching the Augria Sand Dunes. Roughly a year ago, this person had been brought along with the mansion’s household in their move, and kept in the Confinement Room ever since. Not the sort of acquaintance one would ever wish to call on, nor a safe one to call on either—but—

“I really do wonder what kind of results they’ll bring back. To make me hold expectations like these—perhaps theeey’ve swayed me yeet again.”

Roswaal let his back sink into the couch, the words trickling out of him as he sank deeper and deeper. One person had been left behind in the parlor. The sorcerer turned over the lingering traces of the discussion just past, and someone came to mind, and a small laugh escaped him. Then,

“Does this mean I’m becoming one of his favored members, too? What do you think?”

“…You do say the most repulsive things, I suppose. That’s what I think, in fact. You’ve truly no limits, to the point it’s frightening—there’s simply no other way to put it, I suppose.”

“It’s not as though I’m so devoid of principle as to try getting close to him with thiis body.”

“You were a woman before, as well, in fact. Betty is merely keeping a proper guard, I suppose.”

Roswaal’s smile grew all the cheerier, and the little girl who had answered him—Beatrice—began to toy with her own hair, tugging at it, her expression composed. Perhaps it was boredom, or perhaps a habit she fell into when her irritation began to rise.

“Subaru-kun and the others are on their way to the Confinement Room, you know. Don’t you think it’s unsafe, without you byy his side?”

“A stray that bares its fangs is less trouble than a house dog that hones them, in fact. Which is why I thought it best to keep the latter in proper check, I suppose.”

“To be called a house dog is truly cruel. Though to be Teacher’s dog would be something I’d yearn for.”

“You are a degenerate beyond saving, in fact… Roswaal, don’t test Subaru too far, I suppose. Betty won’t allow you to provoke him overmuch, in fact.”

Roswaal let out a smile with a hint of the obscene in it, and was duly rebuked by Beatrice and her sour face. He wiped the smile away and, as ever, closed his one eye—the blue—returning her look with the yellow one alone.

“You and I see things differently. You act without a care, while I act with a single purpose in mind.”

“Which would mean you’re seeing a side of me utterly unlike Mother’s; the Betty who holds Subaru’s hand is hundreds of times the better of the two, I suppose.”

“That is exactly what I’m referring to. It’s well and good for you, who put on the airs of the Guardian of the Forbidden Library, to become as happy as you possibly can.”

“That’s a given, in fact. Which is why I’ll have you permit me to act, in order to protect that happiness, I suppose.”

Roswaal still on the couch, Beatrice standing right before him—with their lines of sight level, sparks flew in every direction. He narrowed his eyes, as if to peer into the heart of the round-eyed girl, and said,

“With one of the Sin Archbishops slain, a second ought to have entered Subaru-kun.”

“…There should be candidates other than Subaru, in fact.”

“But none who come so near him, none who overlap with him. Which one gets chosen is a foregone conclusion—between the one it merely grazes and the one it overlaps. Enough of the dull pretense.”

“—I won’t let you say any more than that, I suppose.”

Beatrice answered his argument with a voice sheathed in a resolve she had kept hidden until now. She gripped the sleeve of her arm tight and glared at him.

“Betty is Subaru’s, in fact. And so Subaru will remain Subaru, I suppose.”

“I’ve no wish to hinder yoour efforts. The lower the number, the more convenient for me. That’s aall.”

“—Tch.”

At the very last, Beatrice threw a scathing look at Roswaal and turned for the door. This was the end of the discussion.

“Beatrice.”

Roswaal called to the girl’s back as she moved away. Beatrice stopped, but did not turn around.

“I want you to be happy—more so than any of the others, at least. Because you are… like a little sister to me.”

“—A truly repulsive thing to say, in fact. And you will never be like Mother, I suppose.”

“Isn’t that just what love iis?”

There was no rebuttal. There was only the sound of the door opening and closing, echoing quietly through the parlor. And after that, no further sound stirred within it.
