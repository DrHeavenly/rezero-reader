# Chapter 1 — The Dragon Carriage’s Way Back

Natsuki Subaru’s party had left the city of Priestella and kept on due east.

They were bound for the eastern edge of the world—the region that held the Augria Sand Dunes, a stretch of land with a long and jealous history. For hundreds of years the Witchbeasts that lived there had turned back every living thing that tried to set foot on their ground.

There was the Miasma, which robbed a traveler of all sense of direction and gnawed away at the heart. There were the packs of Witchbeasts, an evil vanguard that glutted themselves on human life. And there was the one who sought the peace of the world—the one at the Watchtower, who weighed every success and every failure of those who came near. That was the Sage, Shaula.

“Knowing just that much, it feels like we’re lining up to throw ourselves off a cliff.”

Simply being reminded of the obstacles that lay ahead was enough to drag the spirits down. In truth, anyone who traveled to a place like that knowing full well what waited there was either suicidal or a Witch Cultist—one way or another, a person with something wrong in the head.

But these people were neither, and not one of them meant to die. Just the opposite: they meant to live. They meant to hold out a path of life to every person who had lost the way of their own living, and it was to grasp that one seed of hope that they had set out on this expedition.

“…And even so. It’s strange.”

Subaru let the words slip out toward the faces across from him, imagining the sway of the dragon carriage as he said them. At the whisper, every last person in the compartment turned their eyes his way.

Four glances came at him—and adding Subaru to the rest made five passengers riding in the one carriage. That was the shape of the thing.

“Strange? Did something happen?” Emilia asked.

“No, nothing worth mentioning, Emilia-tan. It’s just—welll, it just struck me as pretty unexpected, all these faces sharing the same dragon carriage and getting along this well.” Subaru waved the question off as he said it.

To his right and left, covering both his flanks, sat Emilia and Beatrice. They were his own people, and that the two of them would be at his side went without saying. The real question was the pair up front.

It was Anastasia who answered—a high, clear laugh coming first, the words trailing after it in her fake Kansai, or rather Kararagi, dialect. “Well, it’s only natural ya’d feel like that. Truth is, I thought it was real surprisin’ myself. So it makes sense Natsuki-kun’d feel a bit outta place, hm.”

“…Ah. That is so,” Julius said.

Her reply delivered, Anastasia looked at Subaru with a meaning-laden gaze, toying with a lock of her hair.

“Though if it’s bein’ outta place we’re talkin’ about, I’m not so sure ya can beat Julius-san here.”

“How harsh.” Julius bowed his head. “But please, do not worry on my account—I too am a knight at heart. A single night is enough for me to set my heart in order, and several days have already passed. I promise you will never again see the shameful figure I cut the other day.”

“So gracious. I’ll take ya at yer word, then.”

The way Julius bowed in answer to Anastasia’s teasing was just the way he had always carried himself. And no wonder. He had taken damage from the Authority of Gluttony—but to be precise, the damage had fallen on everyone around him rather than on the man himself. It was his friends, not Julius, who had had their memories and their conduct altered. He could hardly hide how much those reactions had shaken him. Yet, as he had just said himself, he seemed to have accepted it with an easy grace.

“…You’re unbelievable.”

Subaru wouldn’t let his voice carry the praise aloud, but it was no small thing. That weak corner of his heart couldn’t bring itself to say the admiration plainly. And it wasn’t as if the feeling were foreign to him—the feeling of having your own existence left behind, of no one anywhere who shared your memories.

Natsuki Subaru, who could turn back time with Return by Death, most certainly carried the memories of worlds no one else remembered. If he reached for a single instance of building up bonds only to lose them, it would be those first days at the Roswaal mansion—the repeating days at the beginning of everything.

Summoned to another world, he had struggled in the Royal Capital to help Emilia, and as a result had been invited to Roswaal’s mansion. There he had lived through four turns of events before he stepped into the fifth world. The memories of those four worlds were held inside Subaru and nowhere else. Even now it was hard to forget the shock of being treated as a stranger by the very people with whom he had built those bonds during his days at the mansion.

That pain, that sorrow, would never fade.

“…What is it, I wonder? Subaru, why have you started petting Betty’s head all of a sudden, in fact?”

“Don’t mind it. I’m just moved, thinking about how seriously you saved me back then. Thank you, thank you.”

“I don’t understand, I suppose. And my hair’s getting mussed, so sto—you don’t have to stop, but pet it a little more gently, in fact.”

Beatrice tilted her head toward him from where she perched half a seat away. Just as she’d asked, Subaru gave her head a few soft pats, refusing to let himself forget the state of his heart in the moment she had truly saved him.

Beatrice was the one person whose feelings toward Subaru had not shifted through all the looping days at the mansion. They had met before Return by Death’s save point, and so, from then on, she alone had never once changed the way she reached out to him. And the way he had been saved would stay a secret from her, too, for a long, long time.

“After that, it was Emilia-tan who saved me.”

“—?”

Emilia made a puzzled face at Subaru’s reminiscing. Subaru was here, now, because he had been saved by her kindness, her voice, her manner, and the warmth of the lap she had lent him.

“—”

That was exactly why Subaru could recognize how extraordinary it was that Julius had pulled it off entirely on his own. It had taken him so much help, so many tries—four worlds’ worth of time—to climb over what Julius had climbed. Or perhaps it was because there had been a certain someone at Julius’s side, lending a hand.

A certain someone Anastasia had forgotten; a certain someone who asked no help of anyone in his own camp; a certain someone whose ties to his contracted Quasi Spirits had been severed; a certain someone whose own blood brother no longer remembered him. If that was how it had gone, then Subaru was grateful for it.

“You’ve been staring at people’s faces for a while now. Is something the matter, Subaru?”

“I’m not doing anything wrong. I was just thinking it’s not much fun to watch you smile so elegantly right after somebody calls you gracious.”

“Beautifully elegant—now that has a fine ring to it. I never expected to receive praise from you. I’m pleasantly surprised… or, well, perhaps that’s putting it too strongly. It’s only natural, is it not?”

“I wasn’t praising you, and hearing you spin it like that pisses me off!”

With the same manner as before—or rather, with a tongue that seemed to have grown even nimbler at its little slips—Julius left Subaru the one worked up in the end. Back in his brother’s hospital room in Priestella, the plain frailty on Julius’s face had been hard to swallow; Subaru remembered that time, the dissatisfaction written across the man’s expression, and how it had prodded him into complaining. All of that seemed like utterly forgotten history now.

“You and Julius get along. Hmm, were you two like this before, the way you are now?”

“Emilia-tan, you’ve got it all wrong. There’s no way me and this guy get along. Did you see my face? My eyes had the scariest look you’ve ever seen. Well—from my side, anyway.”

“Really? I don’t think you’re right, but… ah, I’m not talking about the look in your eyes. I mean the part where you said you two don’t get along. Subaru’s eyes were the same as ever—reaaally scary.”

“The same as ever, reaaally scary?!”

“For me, though, I like Subaru’s eyes. I’m being truthfully truthful.”

“Ah, dammit. How I wish that bit about my eyes could’ve been cut from the recording…”

Emilia gave a wry smile as she filled Subaru with regret, then turned an inquisitive look on Julius, who returned it with a glance at Subaru.

“I have no room to deny her words,” Julius said. “In truth, to call the two of us close would not be quite correct. And yet, in my eyes, we are meeting each other halfway, however things may appear.”

“‘Meeting halfway’ is already condescending. Come down off it a little—it’s only gonna piss me off more.”

“What an abrasive way of seeing things. Would you tell him the same, Emilia-sama?”

Julius sought Emilia’s agreement, but she only smoothed back her hair and said,

“Just as I thought—you two are reaaally close, aren’t you?”

“Come on. Isn’t it obvious by now?”

“Eh? Well, I suppose you are close, then…”

There was a faint disagreement in Emilia’s tone, but Subaru and Julius carried on as though nothing had happened, which left her more and more baffled. Anastasia, trying to hold her laughter in, let it out anyway in a chain of chuckles.

At her reaction Julius loosened his lips, as if he’d been put at ease—and that fleeting master-and-servant rapport struck Subaru as nothing strange at all, familiar as he was with the other side of it.

The Anastasia here was not the true Anastasia Hoshin. She was a fake, only a fake.

Anastasia’s body was, at present, being borrowed by the Artificial Spirit Echidna—otherwise known as Eridna. This Eridna claimed to have no intention of stealing the body for good, but Subaru couldn’t say how far he could trust that. Being one of the few who knew the truth, the one thing he was careful never to fail at was keeping those who didn’t know—Emilia and Julius among them—from ever noticing.

“…Subaru, cut it out, in fact.”

Beatrice called out to him where he stood lost in thought. The girl’s hair was a mess again, since Subaru had gone on petting her head all through that stretch of silence, and her lovely little face wore a sour look.

“Whoa—?! Sorry, Beako! Your cute look’s a disaster… your hair’s all over the place now.”

“Perhaps you shouldn’t talk as if Betty’s cuteness is ruined by a hairstyle alone, I suppose! And your own face is starting to look frightening, in fact.”

Half a seat away, Beatrice spoke while frantically raking her tangled hair back into place with both hands. Subaru let out an “Oh,” swallowing whatever he’d meant to say, as she gave him a look that plainly judged him hopeless.

“There’s no point making such an overly anxious face, I suppose. Betty will handle it, in fact. If something happens, Betty will notice it far sooner than Subaru will, I suppose.”

“…Yeah.”

Beatrice offered the words in answer to his unease. Beatrice—the one person besides Subaru who knew about Eridna, and how she had taken Anastasia’s place.

Subaru had promised to keep it quiet for two reasons: first, to avoid stirring up needless commotion; second, because Eridna had claimed she meant no theft of Anastasia’s body. Even so, if anything did happen, Beatrice would be the only one able to tell—belatedly, but surely. The two of them were the same kind of Artificial Spirit, two beings bound by a shared tie to the Witch Echidna. If Eridna were plotting something, Beatrice would sense it.

He didn’t much like the idea of tangling Beatrice up with Echidna, if he thought of how she must feel about it—but,

“Set your pointless suspicions aside. It’s fine that Subaru acted like Subaru, in fact—reaching out to anyone at all, never mind who, instead of stewing over it alone, just as you always do, I suppose. Though you did come to Betty first. I’ll grant you my praise this once for that, in fact.”

That was the last word on the matter, so the matter was closed.

“—I can see it.”

Just as Subaru thought the carriage had settled into silence, Emilia murmured the words. She had spotted their destination in the distance, her gaze fixed out the carriage window, and Subaru understood as well that the view outside was turning into scenery he knew.

“What a nostalgic road. We’ll be at Roswaal’s mansion soon, huh.”

He murmured it while reining in the feeling of standing at their first waypoint, calling their real goal back to mind.

The party’s destination, as said before, was the Augria Sand Dunes—the place where they meant to seek out the Sage Shaula. But they had already arranged to stop by Roswaal’s mansion on the way, both to ready themselves and to break the long march of the expedition. There was much they wanted to discuss with the fellow member of their camp: the incident at Priestella, and the matter of speaking with the Sage.

And besides that—

“It’s not just a stop along the road.”

There was meaning in dropping by Roswaal’s mansion before the expedition to the Sage began. Set against the whole of the world, that meaning was slight, a small thing. But through Natsuki Subaru’s small human eyes, it held great possibility.

“—”

Subaru narrowed his eyes, picturing the Roswaal mansion still too far off to see. Each of the four others in the carriage was watching him. Every color of feeling lived in their eyes, but Subaru hadn’t the composure to notice a single one of those glances.

***

By the time the dragon carriage reached Roswaal’s mansion, it was more than an hour past noon.

The long migration from the city of Priestella, some ten days on the road, came to a temporary halt.

“Subaru!”

“Oh—Petra, whoawhoa—?!”

They rode the ground dragons up to the mansion, gave their thanks to the coachman, and climbed down from the carriage. A small maid came flying out of the mansion toward Subaru and threw herself at him with no restraint at all. She struck him full in the chest, and Subaru somehow caught her while stumbling backward, then patted the head of the girl who had launched herself into his arms.

“Petra, you’ll startle me, jumping out like that. I’ve been gone almost a month—have you been keeping well?”

“I’ve been fine, as you can see. But Subaru… -sama, you’ve had it rough, haven’t you? I’ve been worried ever since that letter arrived two days ago. You went and got yourself hurt so badly again, didn’t you? Can you bear the pain?”

“Embarrassing, embarrassing.”

Petra pressed at his body to check for pain, her reddish-brown hair swinging back and forth. As Subaru met it with a wry smile, the others were climbing down from the carriage.

“Hey, Petra. Do your job as a maid of the house properly. If you don’t, Ram and Frederica’ll get after you.”

“…All right. Tell me what happened later.”

She drew back at once, though it was plain her trust in him had taken real damage. Petra’s worry was inevitable, if he told her how he’d been dragged into what amounted to a city-wide uproar over a single crystal—not that Subaru had meant to hand the Witch Cult any such thing. But that excuse would be meaningless too, since the truth was that most of the Cult’s aims traced back to Emilia’s camp.

“Welcome, honored guests. My compliments on the efforts of your long journey. I am Petra Leyte, a maid of this residence. Allow me to show you into the mansion.”

Setting Subaru’s memories aside, Petra delivered the greeting with a dignified little gesture. Julius took Anastasia’s hand to help her down from the carriage, and both of them wore looks of open admiration. On Emilia’s face, and on Beatrice’s, was something almost proud.

“Quite the well-behaved little thing, small as ya are. Yer keepin’ up with yer trainin’, hm.”

“To think she was a wholly different person, the way she flung herself at Subaru at first sight.”

“—How embarrassing.”

Julius fell in with the smiling Anastasia, and Petra’s face went redder and redder at their remarks. He looked down at her, then over at Subaru, and said,

“I see. Now I understand why you’ve earned the title of Lolimancer.”

“For your information, Petra’s not as tiny as some little kid, and even if you call me that, the only loli I’ll ever ‘mance’ is Beako. Get your facts straight.”

“And Betty is not some girl who works hard to be used at Subaru’s convenience, I suppose. Subaru, you’d do well to get your facts straight too, in fact.”

Subaru answered Julius’s needling coldly, and Beatrice rebuked Subaru more coldly still. His mouth twisted into a frown and he fell silent; Beatrice let out a sigh. Then she turned her eyes to Petra’s lower half, stepped up beside her, and made a small motion to catch the hem of the girl’s maid skirt.

“Also—look, not much has changed between Betty and Petra over our time, I suppo… wait a moment. Betty feels as if Petra has grown a little, in fact. Her hair’s grown out a bit too, I suppose…!”

“It’s because a month has gone by, and I’m in my growing season right now. I’ll get taller, and my hair will get longer if I don’t cut it. Beatrice-chan will stay small, though.”

“Wha—what in tarnation, in fact…!”

Petra smiled and pulled the astonished Beatrice into a tight hug. She held the pouting girl for a good long while, then let go and walked off, saying, “Well then, allow me to guide you.”

“All right, we’ll finally head to the—mmgah.”

He’d meant to follow her when a hand seized the back of his collar. Choking out a sound, he looked around, and was startled to find it was Emilia who’d caught him by the scruff. Holding the back of his neck, she jerked his chin toward the dragon carriage behind him.

“Subaru, Patrasche doesn’t look very happy.”

“Hm… this has turned into a selfish little affair, hasn’t it.”

If you followed Emilia’s gaze to the ground dragons—of the two they’d traveled with, the beautiful, jet-black one was glaring at Subaru with a frightfully piercing stare. That was Subaru’s beloved dragon, Patrasche. When he tried to return the look, she turned away flatly and let out a small, deeply displeased sound.

“You really couldn’t make up with her, even with ten whole days, huh.”

“Yeah, I know. But I really don’t think it’s my fault this time…”

Subaru scratched his head, wondering what to do about Patrasche as she leaned into his belly. Ever since they’d left Priestella, Patrasche had been giving him the cold shoulder—or a little before that, really. When he’d gone to the stable to collect her after the defensive battle against the Witch Cult was done and dusted, she’d already been in a foul mood. He hadn’t been able to ask her about it, with Otto out of commission from his injuries. But he’d known Patrasche a long time. It was about time he figured out what his beloved dragon was so angry about.

“That’s—look, I know I neglected you during all the chaos, I know I made you feel lonely, but… guh, gwah—?!”

“Subaru?!”

He was in the middle of the sentence when Patrasche lashed her tail at him, and the direct hit left him coated in dirt. As expected, she’d shown no mercy in her counterattack. Answer her with anything false and she’d strike without a shred of hesitation.

“Never saw it coming… To think I dodged Regulus’s attacks and I can’t dodge this—”

“Are you all right? Patrasche just swung her tail again out of nowhere—doesn’t that mean you’ll get hit once more if you don’t say the right thing?”

Emilia made that terrifying point as she knelt on one knee to lend him a hand. He looked to Patrasche, and there she was, sure enough, swishing her long tail, slicing the air with its tip as if to threaten him. It felt like she was promising to double the force for every mistake he made. Without meaning to he lost the power of speech, his throat cramping as he grasped that he couldn’t afford a careless word.

“For all your carelessness, your ground dragon holds an overwhelming amount of sympathy for you.”

But that remark from Julius, dispirited as he was by the failure to get everyone moving, only risked the unwelcome effect of bringing Patrasche down on Subaru’s head. Subaru pointed back and forth between himself and the dragon and said,

“Really? I’m kinda covered head to toe in dirt here, y’know? You call this sympathy?”

“Can you not see the proof of her deep affection with your own eyes? She is a kind-hearted ground dragon. What galls her most is that she could do nothing to help you in that turmoil, and that you never asked her to. So it may be indignation rather than anger she feels toward you.”

“What are you even saying? Just how high are you gonna crank her heroine power? Cut it out.”

How right Julius was, there was no telling—but at the very least, his words brought the swinging of Patrasche’s tail to a stop. Subaru looked at her again, and she turned away, same as always, but—

“Looking at her now, it seems like she got a little embarrassed after hearing that.”

“Emilia-tan, you noticed too? So did I. It makes my heart flutter, so please don’t do that too often, Patrasche.”

He said it, reached out a nervous hand, and at last Patrasche lowered her head, letting Subaru touch the body armored in tough scales. And with that, it seemed, the cold war he’d had ten days to end came to a close on a good note.

“Isn’t that enough now? Is everything settled?”

Petra let her impatience show through her raised voice; they’d gone on lingering in the mansion’s front garden with no regard for the hour. She’d been sent out to guide them, and here they were, eating up all this time.

“If we don’t get back soon, Ram-neesama will get mad again, and—”

“—If you can spare a thought for things like that, it only means your efforts fell just a little short.”

“Kyaaa—?!”

Petra, whose footsteps had been making light, hurried sounds, let out a shriek and snapped her spine straight at that voice. She turned to find a pink-haired maid coming toward them from the direction of the mansion.

It was Ram, no mistake, no doubt about it. Once Petra had gone as still as a stone statue under her glare, Ram slowly turned her gaze on Subaru’s party and their guests, and—

“Just when I thought Petra had gotten better at her work—to see this, right on the verge of acknowledging her efforts… Ram is truly disappointed.”

“I-I’m sorry, Ram-neesama… my work—you were about to acknowledge it?”

“Yes. Your cooking is better than Ram’s, your cleaning more sensible than Ram’s, you do the laundry better than Ram, and you rise earlier than Ram, so Ram was close to acknowledging you.”

“You should have a little more self-doubt when you’re listing all that off!”

Just how far behind did this veteran maid stand next to a girl with a single year of maid’s experience? Even allowing for how quickly Petra learned, the bar was set so low it wasn’t worth measuring. At his shout, Ram gave a snort—the “hah” Subaru knew far too well—and said,

“How does one go about doubting oneself? Ram holds only expectation and confidence toward herself.”

“You know, that’s the one part of you I’ve always thought really deserves respect.”

Subaru answered her stately declaration half in exasperation. There was, in fact, reason to imitate Ram’s optimism—or rather, her fortitude. He only wished she’d reflect on herself a little more, but—

“Then again, if you did that, I guess you wouldn’t really feel like the real Ram anymore, huh.”

“What a frightening thought. And I don’t think it wise to leave guests standing out here so long. Let us hurry inside.”

Subaru went along with Ram’s brisk dismissal. They’d spent far too long outside the mansion. It wouldn’t be strange for Frederica, or even Roswaal, to come out looking for them if they wasted any more of it. The Royal Candidates and the Margrave were all to gather here, to talk over what came next.

“Is Roswaal at the mansion right now?”

Ram, already walking ahead to lead them, tapped Petra’s shoulder to loosen the girl’s stiffness. At Emilia’s call from behind, she answered “Yes” without looking back.

“Fortunately, he was staying at the residence when the letter arrived, so he has remained here. The letter carried a summary of the Priestella affair… and there should be a great deal to discuss.”

“Yeah, there should be, considering how much went down…”

“There are fewer of you now than when you first set out, aren’t there—did Garf and Otto die?”

“Like hell I’d let them die! They’re not dead! They’re just coming home later because of their injuries!”

Just as Subaru was about to broach the subject of the letter, his charge was turned aside. Ram answered with an indifferent little shrug.

“I know all too well the depths of a man who can’t keep his composure. Barusu’s especially is the sort with a hole punched clean through the bottom, spilling everything he’s got, so do take care. To let people measure your depths and have them find you empty inside—there’s nothing worse.”

“That actually hurts, so knock it off! And besides—”

If the details written in the letter sent ahead to the mansion before they’d left Priestella were what he thought they were, everyone here should already understand that this was no simple trip home for Subaru and the others. So when Subaru tried to confirm the second half of that letter, choosing his words as he settled on them, Ram promptly raised a finger.

“I told you, didn’t I? Rest easy. Roswaal-sama is here at the mansion… and preparations have been made for both the Sleeping Beauty and the Confinement Room. Although—”

Ram broke off at the end and gently licked her lips. She wet them with the red tip of her tongue, a gesture with some trace of grace worked into it, and said,

“Although neither of those was Ram’s doing. That was Frederica and Petra’s work.”

“Why’d you put on such a proud face for that part?”

In any case, even with a whole month gone since he’d last seen her, Ram was exactly the same as ever.
